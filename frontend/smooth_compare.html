How far surface smoothing can go (interactive)

This interactive comparison studies the surface-quality lever for generated chairs. It shows the raw reconstruction alongside the mesh after 16 and after 40 iterations of volume-preserving Taubin smoothing. The large improvement comes from the initial cleanup-and-graft; moving from 16 to 40 iterations yields only a marginal gain, because at the delivered ~15,000-face budget it is the polygon count, not the smoothing, that limits surface quality. The figure motivates treating smoothing and face-budget together rather than smoothing alone.

Why it exists & what it documents: After the base graft the surface still read as lumpy, so we tested whether more smoothing was the answer. It fixed that assumption — showing the face budget, not smoothing, is the limit — and documents why smoothing was left at a modest setting rather than pushed further.
