1

Photo → 3D — five AI engines make the mesh app tab: ⚙️ Generate object

Drop a single furniture photo in the Generate object tab: the image is segmented (rembg), a 3D engine reconstructs the mesh (TripoSR runs locally on this laptop; TripoSG, TRELLIS, SAM 3D and InstantMesh ran on cloud GPUs), the result is cleaned, real-dimensioned and named with a professional code like desk-SAM3D-001. You can force the object type, pick chair-base styles, and install stronger engines from inside the app if the machine can hold them.

Benchmarks — which AI wins, measured not guessed evidence for: ⚙️ Generate object

3

Repair & refine — broken meshes become products runs inside: ⚙️ Generate object

Raw AI output is rarely usable: swivel chairs come out with ~500-fragment broken bases, surfaces are noisy, exports are bloated. This step grafts parametric bases, smooths, decimates and optimizes the IFC — the difference between a demo and a deliverable.

4

The Catalog — 626 generated items + ABO + your own app tabs: ⚙️ Generate · 🛏️ Build a room

Everything that survives steps 1–3 lands in the catalog with a professional code — item · engine · number (desk-SAM3D-001, sofa-TRL-014…), renumbered automatically when you delete. Today: TRELLIS 197 · TripoSG 194 · SF3D 192 · TripoSR 20 · InstantMesh 10 · SAM 3D 10 · CC0 3, plus the 38-category ABO pool with real Neufert dimensions. You can also declare a custom category and upload your own IFC items — they join the same numbering and become placeable everywhere.

5

Rooms — human logic + German law place the furniture app tab: 🛏️ Build a room

A CP-SAT solver places every item under the ASR (Arbeitsstättenrichtlinie) envelope — legal staffing caps, movement areas, route widths — and the human relational rules: chair tucked at its desk, monitor facing the sitter, bin at arm's reach, stools ringing tables, projector on the ceiling aimed at the screen, coat rack flanking the door, planters in corners. Item counts scale with room size; anything that can't sit legally is refused, never forced.

📋 Item Logic Register — the rulebook in tables All 38 items: distribution logic, purpose, and standing vs the German standard — ASR-cited (green), stricter-than-ASR (blue), ergonomic practice (grey). Also in the repo as docs/ITEM_LOGIC_REGISTER.md and locally as Word. 🎛️ Live Layout Visualizer — solve a room right now Pick a room type (office-first), a size and a population level — the real CP-SAT + ASR engine lays it out in front of you: 2D clearance plan + 3D render, honest refusals included. 🛋️ The Showroom — every item in one room A 16×12 m office holding one of each of the 38 catalog categories (41 pieces, dining ring + desk cluster patterns) — orbit the 3D scene next to the solver's numbered 2D clearance plan. 🛋️ Human Layouts — dense · denser · densest Six room types at three escalating densities through the real solver: activity-based zoning, relational placement and the full ASR envelope. 18 scenarios, 2D + 3D side by side.
6

Buildings — whole IFC buildings, furnished floor by floor app tab: 🏢 Building

Drop any IFC on the Building tab — drop-tested twice on 2026-07-15 with a fresh 7-storey tower: 9/9 space-bearing floors, 81/81 rooms exact, smart suggestions attached, and the full catalog (including your own generated items, picked as gen:<id>) usable per room (reproducible: scripts/drop_test_7f.py, report in docs/DROP_TEST_REPORT.md). Floors are dissected, every room gets the same room logic as step 5 — de-rotated solve frames, walking-path protection, door keep-clears, per-room blueprints, Light/Medium/Dense — then export the furnished building back to IFC/GLB for Revit.
