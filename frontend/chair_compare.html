TripoSR vs. a stronger model — where reconstruction quality actually comes from

This figure isolates the two levers that govern single-photo 3D reconstruction quality in the SCS photo-to-BIM pipeline. Panel 1 shows the raw mesh produced by TripoSR from one photograph of the chair. Panel 2 shows the identical mesh after the project's post-processing stack: connected-component debris removal, volume-preserving Taubin smoothing, and Garland-Heckbert quadric decimation. Panel 3 shows the same chair reconstructed by a stronger generative model (SAM 3D, benchmark F-score 0.63). Comparing 1 to 2 demonstrates that cleanup barely alters the result, while 2 to 3 reveals that the decisive quality gain comes from the generative model itself. The conclusion driving the pipeline design is that model selection, not mesh post-processing, is the primary determinant of reconstruction fidelity.

Why it exists & what it documents: We needed to decide where to invest — in cleaning up TripoSR output or in adopting a heavier model — before committing the pipeline's engineering effort. This was generated after a five-generator benchmark to make that tradeoff visible on a real chair, and it is kept as the evidence for the project's core design decision: prioritise model choice over mesh cleanup.
