Ergonomic proportioning of the grafted chair base

Having established that a parametric base can be grafted onto a generated chair, this figure tunes its size. The three renders show the same chair with the base footprint set to 0.55, 0.42, and 0.34 of the seat width. At 0.55 the base splays about 20% wider than the seat (unstable and visually wrong); at 0.34 it is stubby and under-supportive; at 0.42 it sits within the seat footprint and reads as a correct office-chair base. The chosen 0.42 ratio encodes an ergonomic proportion directly into the procedural base generator.

Why it exists & what it documents: The first grafted base splayed wider than the seat and looked wrong, so we generated three sizes to fix the proportion empirically. It documents why the base radius is fixed at 0.42 of the seat width in the shipped generator.
