Repairing TripoSR's fragmented chair base with a parametric CAD graft

Single-photo reconstruction consistently fails on thin, partially occluded, spoke-like geometry: TripoSR renders an office chair's rolling five-star wheelbase as a cloud of fragmented, floating pieces. The left panel shows this raw failure; the right panel shows the project's remedy. The good, generated seat and back are retained, the broken base region is cut away, and a clean parametric CAD five-star base (hub, gas column, five spokes, five casters) is grafted on and fused into a single watertight solid. This hybrid generated-mesh-plus-parametric-part strategy recovers a physically correct chair where pure reconstruction cannot.

Why it exists & what it documents: The fragmented wheelbase was the most visible reconstruction failure and was flagged directly by the client, so we needed both to show the failure and to prove the parametric graft repairs it. It documents the hybrid generated-plus-CAD base repair that ships in the app for office chairs.
