A uniform optimization budget across the furniture library

This gallery renders ten reconstructed furniture items after the full cleanup and IFC-optimization stack. Each caption reports the face-count reduction from the raw reconstruction to the delivered asset: meshes ranging from roughly 50,000 to 126,000 triangles are all reduced to a uniform ~15,000-triangle, ~264 KB budget through debris removal, smoothing, and quadric decimation, while remaining recognizable. The already-lightweight lamp (12,488 to 12,396) shows the decimation floor, where the input is already below budget and simplification yields no benefit, motivating an adaptive rather than a fixed target.

Why it exists & what it documents: We needed to confirm a single optimization budget works across wildly different mesh complexities and to catch cases where it does not. It surfaced the decimation floor (the already-small lamp), which is why the documentation recommends an adaptive rather than a fixed face target.
