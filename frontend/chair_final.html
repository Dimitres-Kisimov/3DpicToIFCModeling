From raw reconstruction to a BIM-ready asset

This viewer presents the final, optimized office-chair mesh delivered into IFC. The raw TripoSR reconstruction (85,500 triangles, 1.5 MB) is reduced to 15,000 triangles and 264 KB — an 82% reduction in faces and 83% in file size — through debris removal, Taubin smoothing, quadric decimation, and colour preservation via a PBR base-colour factor. The result retains the recognizable chair while being light enough to embed in a building information model, illustrating the fidelity-versus-payload tradeoff every generated asset must satisfy before IFC delivery.

Why it exists & what it documents: BIM tools choke on raw multi-hundred-thousand-face reconstructions, so we had to prove a generated chair could be made light enough to embed in IFC without losing its identity. It documents the delivered optimization target (~15k faces / ~264 KB) and is the reference for what "BIM-ready" means in this project.
