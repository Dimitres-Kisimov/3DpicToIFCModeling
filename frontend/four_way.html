Four representations of one chair: raw, optimized, catalog, and mesh-only

This four-panel comparison places, side by side, four ways the same photographed office chair can enter a BIM model. Panel 1 is the raw TripoSR mesh (85,500 faces, 1.5 MB). Panel 2 is that mesh after the IFC optimizer (optimize_ifc: decimation, geometry instancing, precision rounding) — about 15,000 faces in a 423 KB IFC. Panel 3 is a clean catalog chair retrieved by visual similarity (SAM 3D) — visually clean but a different chair than the one photographed. Panel 4 is the same cleanup as panel 2 but delivered as a GLB (clean_and_optimize, 264 KB). Panels 2 and 4 are the identical geometry pipeline in an IFC versus GLB container. The figure makes explicit the two central tradeoffs of the system: fidelity versus cleanliness, and reconstruction versus catalog retrieval.

Why it exists & what it documents: Reviewers kept conflating "optimized", "catalog", and "raw", so we built one screen putting all four side by side to settle what each actually costs and preserves. It fixed that confusion by showing IFC- and GLB-optimization are the same geometry and that catalog retrieval trades identity for cleanliness; it documents the option space for how an object enters the model.
